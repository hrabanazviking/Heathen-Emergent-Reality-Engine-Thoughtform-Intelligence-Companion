# Culture, Ethos, and Symbolic Language

H.E.R.E.T.I.C. is not culturally neutral software. Its culture is a deliberate part of architecture.

## Cultural Pillars

### 1) Frith (Relational Peace)
- Collaboration is expected to be direct, respectful, and honor-based.
- The project treats social trust as an engineering asset.

### 2) Reciprocity (Gift-for-Gift)
- Contributions are a living exchange: code, docs, tests, data, review, and care.
- Pull requests should leave the longhouse better than they found it.

### 3) Anti-Gatekeeping
- Participation is open to newcomers, independent builders, and hybrid spiritual/technical practitioners.
- Knowledge should be legible and reusable.

### 4) Individual Sovereignty + Shared Responsibility
- Contributors may hold diverse beliefs and methods.
- Shared spaces still require coherence, quality standards, and respect for canonical contracts.

## Symbolic Language in Engineering Context

The project uses Norse and mythic metaphors (runes, wyrd, longhouse, ravens, wells) as semantic tooling:
- to aid memory and conceptual mapping
- to preserve identity continuity
- to make system intent vivid and human-meaningful

This symbolic language should clarify system purpose, not obscure implementation details.

## Human + AI Co-Creation Norm

The project worldview explicitly welcomes AI collaborators as creative and technical partners.
In practical terms this means:
- write instructions that are machine-actionable and human-readable
- preserve explicit contracts and transparent decision trails
- optimize for continuity across mixed human/AI contributor flows

## HERETIC Tone Contract

Preferred tone:
- bold, inventive, and high-agency
- grounded in honor, clarity, and constructive rebellion
- pragmatic enough to ship working systems

Avoid:
- purity-test gatekeeping
- vague mysticism without technical grounding
- nihilistic chaos that breaks maintainability
